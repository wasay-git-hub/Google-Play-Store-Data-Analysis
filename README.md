# Google Play Store Data Analysis

## Project Overview
This project involves performing **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on the Google Play Store dataset to extract meaningful insights about mobile applications. The analysis focuses on understanding trends across **app categories, pricing, installs, and revenue**, helping to guide data-driven decisions in the app market.

## Objectives
- Identify trends and patterns in app categories.
- Analyze the relationship between app pricing and installs.
- Investigate revenue distribution across different app types.
- Highlight insights using visualizations for better decision-making.

## Key Features
- **Data Cleaning:** Handling missing values, duplicates, and incorrect data types.
- **Exploratory Analysis:** Summarizing key statistics and identifying trends.
- **Visualizations:**
  - **Bar Charts:** To compare app categories and their popularity.
  - **Box Plots:** To analyze distribution of app prices and ratings.
  - **Scatterplots:** To explore relationships between installs, ratings, and revenue.

## Dataset
- Source: Google Play Store dataset.
- Contents: App names, categories, ratings, reviews, sizes, installs, prices, and revenue estimates.

## Tools & Technologies
- Python
- Pandas & NumPy (data manipulation)
- Matplotlib and Plotly (data visualization)
- Jupyter Notebook (analysis environment)

## Insights
- Certain app categories dominate in terms of installs and revenue.
- Pricing affects the number of installs, with most successful apps being free or low-cost.
- Patterns in ratings and reviews provide indicators of app performance and popularity.
